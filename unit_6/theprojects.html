Text-Based Projects

The Exotic Rissole

This short story explores a number of themes and ideas, such as: mutliculturalism and belonging in Australia. Read the short story on pages .. - .. from "Things A Map Won't Show You", then answer the following questions.

  1. What are the names of the 2 main characters? Describe them in detail. What do they look like? Where are they from? What are their strengths?
  2. What is Multiculturalism? You'll need to research this on the internet.
  3. Make a list of 10 unfamilar words from the text. Find the definition using this website: http://www.dictionary.com. For each word, find 3 synonyms. Lastly, for each word, make your own sentence to show your understanding of its meaning and use.
  4. What was the complication (problem) of the story?
  5. How did the story end?
  6. What do you think is the moral (message) of the story?
  7. Would you recommend this story to someone else? Who? Why?
  8. What new experiences have you had since coming to Australia? Have you tried any new foods, engaged in any new activities, made any new friends? Prepare a 2-4 minute speech about your multicultural experiences in Australia.

Only A Game

What is 'sportsmanship'? Is it more important to 'win' in a game or is it being part of 'team' that is more important? These are the sorts of questions that you will explore in this interesting short story about a game of cricket in a place you would least expect - a Detention Centre. Read the story and then answer the following questions.

  1. Using http://www.dictionary.com, look up the following words and find their definitions:
    • sportsmanship
    • competition
    • Find at least 3 more unfamiliar or interesting words from the text.
  2. Who is the main character? Describe him/her.
  3. What is a Detention Centre? You will have to research this online.
  4. What is the difference between first and third person? Which one is used in the story? Can you give an exmaple?
  5. Who won the game? Why?
  6. Make a timeline of key events in this story in chronological order.
  7. Imagine you are one of the following characters and describe how you felt about the game: Lan Nguyen, Akram, or Mr Thistlton. Present your reflect in 2-4 minute monologue.
  8. Write a 200 - 400 word essay about the following prompt: Winning and succeeding is the most important thing in life. Do you agree or disgree? Use examples from the text 'Only a Game' to support your answer as well as other examples.
